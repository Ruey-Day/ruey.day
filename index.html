About Me

I am a dedicated robotics engineer and AI enthusiast, currently pursuing a BSE in Robotics with a minor in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor (GPA: 3.9/4.0). My coursework spans advanced topics such as Robot Operating Systems, Active SLAM, Electronic Circuits, and Data Structures, complemented by certifications in Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing, and SQL.

With a strong passion for innovation, I’ve gained hands-on experience through various roles, including a Robotic Engineering Internship at Stryker, where I designed an autonomous hospital stretcher to optimize caregiver resources. As a Research Assistant in the Electrical and Computer Engineering department, I contributed to enhancing perceptually enabled task guidance by conducting experiments, troubleshooting systems, and fine-tuning AI retrieval models. Additionally, I’ve developed leadership skills as an Orientation Leader, organizing large-scale events and fostering community engagement for new students at the University of Michigan.

My technical skillset includes programming in C++, Python, and Java, as well as proficiency in tools like ROS, Docker, and Unity. I am committed to leveraging my expertise to create innovative solutions in robotics and artificial intelligence.

About Me

I am a robotics engineer focused on Computer Vision and Embedded Systems, currently pursuing a BSE in Robotics with a minor in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. My coursework includes topics in EECS and robotics, complemented by Coursera certifications in Data science and database.

I've gained hands-on experience through various roles, including designing an autonomous hospital stretcher for Stryler with my design team to optimize caregiver resources. As a Research Assistant in the Electrical and Computer Engineering department, I conducted research for task guidance through AI assistance. I'm also part of the Umich Autonomous Robotics Vechical project team's embedded system subteam, where we build and optimize the hardware of our autonomous vehicles.

Aside from my technical skills, I've developed strong communication skills as an Orientation Leader, organizing large-scale welcoming events and leading the campus tour for incoming students at the University of Michigan. Additionally, I'm a native speaker of both English and Taiwanese Mandarin.
